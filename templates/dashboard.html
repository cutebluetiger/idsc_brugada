Clinical Context: Brugada Syndrome

What is Brugada Syndrome?

An inherited cardiac channelopathy causing characteristic ST-segment elevations in right precordial leads (V1–V3). Associated with risk of ventricular fibrillation and sudden cardiac death, particularly during rest or sleep.

Why AI-Assisted Screening?

The ECG pattern can be intermittent and subtle. Expert electrophysiology interpretation is not universally available. This tool flags recordings that warrant closer expert review — it does not diagnose.

Key Leads

The model focuses on leads V1, V2, V3 (right precordial — classic Brugada territory), II (inferior reference), and V4 (anterior transition). This matches established clinical knowledge.

Interpreting the Score

Confidence above 58.4% triggers a Brugada classification. The model was tuned for 80% recall (catches 4 out of 5 cases) while maintaining 87.9% specificity (low false alarm rate).
